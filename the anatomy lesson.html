The Netherlands had a tradition of anatomy lectures, established by the pioneer anatomist Andreas Vesalius (1514-64). Once a year the Praelector Anatomiae, the head of the surgeon's guild would hold a lecture for the members of his guild during which the corpse of an executed criminal would be dissected. On such an occasion it was customary for the Praelector to have his portrait painted, along with other members in attendance. Doctor Nicolaes Tulp (1593-1674) was Praelector from 1628 to 1653 and the painting captures the dissection lecture which he delivered on 31 January 1632.
