THE RISE OF THE CARTHAGINIAN EMPIRE
JOEEPH MALLORD WILLIAM TURNER

  • Painting: The Rise of the Carthaginian Empire

  • Date: 1815

  • Other Name: Dido building Carthage

  • Artist: Joseph Mallord William Turner

  • Medium: Oil on canvas

  • Inspiration: landscape taken from Virgil's Aeneid

  • First Exhibited : Mauritshuis, The Hague

. Turner’s painting of the North African city of Carthage,
founded by Dido, its first queen, was inspired by Virgil’s
epic poem, the Aeneid.

The Rise of the Carthaginian Empire

The figure on the left dressed in blue and wearing a diadem is Dido herself, visiting the tomb that is being built for her dead husband, Sychaeus. The man in a cloak and helmet standing before her is probably Aeneas, the hero of the poem, with whom she will fall in love. Turner painted ten major paintings on the subject of Carthaginian empire. The story of the rise and fall of empires was a theme that preoccupied him throughout his life. This is the first of Turner’s paintings in which he set out to match the seventeenth-century French landscape painter, Claude, in particular Claude’s Seaport paintings.
